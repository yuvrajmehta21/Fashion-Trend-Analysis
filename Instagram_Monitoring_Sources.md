# Style Island — Instagram Trend Monitoring: Proposed Sources

**Purpose:** We're building a private trend-analysis system that watches Instagram each
week to help us **spot rising garment trends 1–2 seasons before they're obvious** — so
we can plan collections from real signal, not guesswork.

**This document is for your input.** Below is the starting list of accounts and hashtags
we propose to monitor. Please **add the brands, designers, and creators you most want
tracked**, and flag any here you'd remove.

---

## How we chose these (the important bit)

To catch trends *early*, we deliberately weight the list toward **trend-leaders** — the
people and brands who adopt looks first — rather than our direct competitors (who, like
us, are *reacting* to trends, so they signal late). Each source has a role:

- **Leading signal** — early adopters: international aspirational brands + influencers.
  International styles tend to reach the Indian market months later, so they give us lead
  time. *This is where early trends show up first.*
- **Confirmation** — direct competitors. We watch them to confirm a trend is reaching our
  exact market, not to discover it.

The system reads the **clothing** in each post (silhouette, colour, neckline, sleeve,
pattern, fabric) and tracks which attributes are gaining engagement over time. **We
analyse garments only — never people, faces, or identities.**

### A note on what this can and can't do (so expectations are right)

This is **decision-support, not a crystal ball.** It will systematically surface rising
attributes and cross-check them against what competitors are stocking and what people are
searching — catching things we'd likely miss by scrolling manually, and giving us
evidence behind buying calls. It won't *guarantee* the next hit; engagement isn't sales.
Realistic goal: **reliably catch 1–2 real trends a season early**, improving each season
as the data builds. Cost to run: a few cents per week.
